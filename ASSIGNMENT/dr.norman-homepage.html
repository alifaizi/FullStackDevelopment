Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Dr. Norman Borlaug, third from the left, trains biologists in Mexico on
how to increase wheat yields - part of his life-long war on hunger.

Here's a timeline of Dr. Borlaug's life

    • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Iowa
    • 1933 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of
      Minnesota,       thanks to a Depression era program known as the
      "National Youth Adminstration"
    • 1970 - reveives the Nobel Peace Prize
    • 1984 - becomes a distinguished professor at Texas A&M
    • 2009 - dies at the age of 95.
